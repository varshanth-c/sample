INDIAN SOCIETY FOR TECHNICAL EDUCATION

Welcome to the ISTE-PESCE Chapter, a branch of the esteemed Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE), New Delhi. Dedicated to advancing engineering education and fostering professional development, our chapter has been a beacon of excellence since its inception in 1989. As an institutional member of ISTE, we are committed to modernizing technical education through curriculum enhancements, faculty development, and infrastructure upgrades. Join us in our journey of growth, innovation, and collaboration as we shape the future of technical education in India.

Path ahead.


  • Vision

    The Indian Society for Technical Education (ISTE) envisions advancing education, particularly in engineering, by enhancing the quality of technical education and research in India to achieve global standards.

  • Mission

    ISTE's mission is to serve as a common agency for engineering educators and administrators, facilitating the development of strategies to upgrade and modernize curriculum, faculty, and educational infrastructure. It aims to improve the overall quality of technical education in India while providing opportunities for professional development to its members.

  • Goals

    The goals and objectives of ISTE are aimed at enhancing the quality and relevance of engineering education in India. This includes ensuring that the curriculum remains updated and valuable, supporting professional development opportunities for engineering educators to improve their teaching skills, upgrading educational infrastructure such as labs and facilities to provide better learning environments, continually assessing and improving the overall quality of technical education, and offering members avenues for ongoing professional growth through events and workshops.

  • Plans

    To achieve these goals, ISTE employs a strategic plan focused on collaboration, innovation, organization, advocacy, and technology integration. This involves fostering partnerships with industry, government, and educational institutions to exchange knowledge and undertake collaborative projects, encouraging innovation among members to explore new ideas and methodologies, maintaining a well-structured and efficient organizational framework to support the club's activities, advocating for the importance of engineering education and garnering support for its advancement, and leveraging technology to facilitate communication, knowledge sharing, and collaboration among members. Through these strategies and tactics, ISTE endeavors to realize its vision of enhancing engineering education and fostering continuous improvement in the field.

Indian Society for Technical Education is a professional organization of engineering educators and educational administrators. It is a society registered under the societies Registration Act XXI of 1860. The principle objective of ISTE is to advance the cause of education in general and all its functions which pertain to engineering and also to serve as common agency for simulating and guiding its members to formulate strategies for development of technical education and research. ISTE had been engaged in a variety of programmes and actions leading to the upgradation and modernization of present day curriculum, faculty and educational infrastructure so as to make the technical education in India comparable with that in the advanced countries. PES College of Engineering, Mandya became Institutional Member of ISTE in the year 1986. ISTE-PESCE Chapter, which is a branch of Indian Society for Technical Education, New Delhi, took birth in the PES College of Engineering campus in the year 1989. Almost all the staff members of the institution are life members of ISTE. The members are benefited through attending short term courses and other activities of the ISTE. Since the beginning of the chapter, many activities have been conducted in the institution for the benefit of staff members and students. A few Programs was also conducted for the family members like short tour, get together, games to spouses and children etc.,
